Found a quick way to look up everything about Telegram accounts — and built a bot for it.

@usergtidbot — a bot that instantly returns the **Telegram ID** of any user, group, or channel. But it does a lot more than that.

What it can do:

— **Native picker buttons** — no need to forward messages or copy usernames. Tap a button, pick a contact, get the result
— **Datacenter detection** — shows which Telegram DC stores the account (DC1-DC5)
— **Account age** — estimates registration date from ID ranges + badges (OG, Veteran, Newcomer)
— **Visual ID card** — generates a PNG card with avatar, ID, datacenter, and age badge
— **Birthday & emoji status** — shows birthday and current custom emoji status (Premium)
— **Collectible usernames** — displays all active usernames, including those purchased on Fragment
— **Personal channel & business profile** — linked channel, business location, and working hours
— **Forward privacy & voice/video** — account restriction flags
— **Profile color** — custom accent color (Premium feature)
— **Profile photos** — total profile photo count
— **Bio & scam detection** — "About" text and Telegram's scam/fake flags
— **Username history** — tracks username changes over time
— **Inline mode** — type @usergtidbot in any chat to get IDs without switching to the bot
— **QR codes** with account ID
— **Compare accounts** — who registered first, age difference
— **Export** — JSON and CSV for integrations and scripts

For bot developers and group admins, this is a daily driver. When you're setting up webhooks, writing a bot, or managing group permissions, you need IDs constantly. Plus the full account profile with DC, age, business info, and username history is a bonus no other bot offers.
